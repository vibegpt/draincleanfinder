Drain Cleaning Guide

What Is Drain Cleaning and When Do You Need It?

Drain cleaning is the professional clearing of blockages, buildup, and intrusions in residential or commercial drain and sewer lines. It covers everything from a single clogged kitchen sink to a full main sewer line backup affecting an entire home.

The two most common methods are drain snaking — using a rotating cable to break through a blockage — and hydro jetting, which uses high-pressure water to scour the inside of the pipe wall clean. Which method is right depends on the type and location of the blockage.

Signs You Need a Pro (Not a DIY Fix)

  • Multiple drains slow at the same time — usually a main line issue
  • Sewage smell coming from drain openings
  • Gurgling sounds when flushing toilets
  • Recurring clogs in the same drain within weeks
  • Water backing up into shower when running washing machine
  • Basement floor drain backing up during heavy rain

How to Choose a Drain Cleaning Company

  • Verify they hold a valid state plumbing or drain contractor license
  • Confirm they carry general liability insurance
  • Get a written quote before any work begins
  • Ask whether the quote covers full clearing or just snaking
  • Check Google reviews for your specific city or neighborhood
  • Avoid companies that quote very low on the phone and add charges on-site

Standard Drain Snaking

A rotating cable breaks through the blockage and pulls it out. Best for hair, grease, and single-fixture clogs. Fast and affordable.

$100 – $300

Hydro Jetting

High-pressure water scours the entire pipe wall. Best for recurring clogs, grease-heavy lines, root intrusion, and older pipe.

$350 – $650

CCTV Camera Inspection

A waterproof camera travels the pipe to diagnose exactly what's causing the problem — before any clearing work begins.

$150 – $350

Main Sewer Line Clearing

Full clearing of the lateral from house to street main. The right fix when multiple fixtures drain slowly or back up at once.

$250 – $500

Emergency Drain Service

Urgent after-hours call-outs including nights, weekends, and holidays. After-hours surcharge typically $75–$150.

$300 – $600+

Commercial Drain Cleaning

Restaurants, offices, and industrial facilities. High-capacity equipment, grease trap service, and preventive maintenance plans.

$200 – $800+

Chemical cleaners can clear minor surface clogs but have real downsides: they can damage older cast-iron or clay pipes, leave a residue that accelerates future buildup, and do nothing for root intrusion or structural pipe problems. For any recurring clog, main line issue, or backup involving sewage, call a professional. It's faster and cheaper long-term than cycling through chemical treatments.
Drain cleaning specialists focus specifically on blockage clearing and drain maintenance — snaking, hydro jetting, CCTV inspection, and sewer line clearing. General plumbers handle a wider range of work including pipe repair, fixture installation, water heaters, and more. For a clogged or slow drain, a drain cleaning specialist is usually faster to dispatch and cheaper than a general plumber.
For most residential homes: kitchen sink drains benefit from cleaning every 1–2 years if you use a garbage disposal regularly. Main sewer laterals should be scoped every 3–5 years in newer homes, or annually if you have mature trees in the yard or a history of root issues. Older homes (pre-1970) with clay or cast-iron lines benefit from annual preventive cleaning. Commercial kitchens typically need quarterly or bi-annual drain maintenance.
Yes — root intrusion is one of the most common causes of sewer backups in homes with mature trees nearby. Roots from cottonwood, willow, elm, and silver maple trees in particular can extend 80–100 feet from the trunk and actively seek moisture inside sewer pipes. They enter through microscopic joint gaps and grow until they cause a full or partial blockage. A CCTV inspection will confirm root intrusion before you spend money on clearing.
